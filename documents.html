The Internship Report usually consists of 3 parts and written in present tense:
  1. The course and company of internship
    2. In the first part you indicate the period and place (address) of the internship and introduce the company.
    • Your name and contact details, Name of your university
    • Name and address of the company and internship
    • Period of the internship and amount of the full working days
    • Introduction of the company (category, field(s) of business, products, services, number of employees, history, development, core competencies, competitors, clients, objectives)
  2. The classification and content of the internship
    3. In the second part you introduce the content of the internship.
    • Department and team of your internship?
    • What did a typical day look like during your internship?
    • Which tasks were assigned to you? Goals?
    • Give concrete examples of what you have done?
    • How was your performance measured?
    • How have you been supervised by the employees and the boss?
    • Were you allowed to work independently and take responsibility?
    • Which of the tasks were easy for you, and which were difficult?
    • What did you learn, both professionally and personally?
    • Which areas were of particular interest to you?
    • In which professional direction would you go further?
    • What were your working hours?
    • Under what conditions did you work?
    • How was the relationship with the employees?
  3. The evaluation and conclusion of the internship
    4. In the third you reflect, evaluate the internship draw conclusions about the role the internship plays in your professional future.
    • What did you gain from the internship?
    • Did it meet your expectations?
    • Do you see any benefit for your career aspirations or your studies?
    • What did you learn in IBS that could be applied in internship?
    • What did you learn in the internship that can be used for IBS?
    • Any ‚Äúgap‚Äù in IBS you could identify and recommend to close?
    • Did the internship arouse your interest in working in this field?
    • What did you learn that can be useful for your future career?
    • What did you like, what not so much?
    • Further opportunities for collaboration (working student, thesis)?
    • Bibliography

Notes from Seniors

  • The report is recommended to write during the intership period, as some pictures or screenshorts might be useful and required in your report.
  • There should be the "Confidentiality Notice" page included, due to the Confidentiality of company data.
  • Citation is required as per the scientific writing method.
  • The Report range is only 2-4 pages long.

The internship paper is a scientific paper and not a report, not an essay, not a narrative review!

The paper is worked out during the internship and can be finalized during or after the internship. The internship paper is dedicated to a topic of the student‚Äôs choice and is supervised by a professor of the faculty of Business Information Systems. The internship paper is not graded, but valued with 3 ECTS credits.

The paper can be conducted in two alternative forms:

  1. Concept, proof of concept, test, research, analysis, evaluation, experiment or solution (based on primary data) developed to solve a given problem or address a specific demand which appeared during the internship.
  2. Structured literature review (based on secondary data) dedicated to a research question related to the topic and content of the internship.

The Internship Report usually consists of 3 parts and written in present tense:

    2. In the first part you indicate the period and place (address) of the internship and introduce the company.

2Ô∏è‚É£ Internship Paper

The internship paper is a scientific paper and not a report, not an essay, not a narrative review!

The paper is worked out during the internship and can be finalized during or after the internship. The internship paper is dedicated to a topic of the student‚Äôs choice and is supervised by a professor of the faculty of Business Information Systems. The internship paper is not graded, but valued with 3 ECTS credits.

The paper can be conducted in two alternative forms:

  1. Concept, proof of concept, test, research, analysis, evaluation, experiment or solution (based on primary data) developed to solve a given problem or address a specific demand which appeared during the internship.
  2. Structured literature review (based on secondary data) dedicated to a research question related to the topic and content of the internship.

4Ô∏è‚É£ Internship Certificate

It is recommended to request the internship certificate of employment three weeks before the internship ends.

At the end of the internship semester (not after your job), the company (employer) shall issue a certificate of employment ‚Äì which is an activity record showing the type and content of the activity, the beginning and end of the internship period, and any absences.

  • Based on the internship report and the proof of activity (certificate of employment), a decision is made (by the dean of studies) whether the student has successfully completed the practical part.
  • If the practical part of the internship is not recognized as successfully completed, it may be repeated once.
  • During an internship semester, the internship site (company) can only be changed in justified exceptional cases with the approval of the Faculty Examination Board.

For a recognized internship semester the student must provide evidence of at least 95 days of attendance (full working days) in a company or in another institution of professional practice after deduction of any days of absence.

The content of the internship certificate of employment may be written in English or German and includes:

  • Heading ("Internship certificate", "Praktikumszeugnis").
  • Personal data of the student trainee including full name, date and place of birth, place and period of employment.
  • Specification of the area in which the internship was completed.
  • Description of the internship employer, for example on the basis of a job description and key economic data.
  • Description of typical tasks during the internship, including mention of particularly important aspects.
  • Evaluation of the student trainee‚Äôs performance and assessment of his/her social behavior towards superiors, colleagues and customers.
  • Closing formula with thanks and wishes for the future.
  • Date, signature of the issuer.

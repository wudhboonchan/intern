4

Citing references! is the key of making your literature review not a copy paper. Let me give you some short summary of the citations we learned from Prof. Rawe.
  • Harvard style

    • Harvard style refencing is practiced as an ‚Äòauthor-date‚Äò style. The in-text citation can be in brackets in the body of the text. Only the author's surname, date of publication and page number are given. Full details are listed in the bibliography.

    In-text citation in the body of the text:
    Empirical study results show that overall rating, variation and volume of consumer reviews are significantly,positively associated with hotel performance. (Xie and Zhang, 2014, p. 1, 6)

    Refencing with full details of the source in bibliography:
    Xie, K.L. and Zhang, Z. and Zhang, Z. (2014). The business value of online consumer reviews and management response to hotel performance, in: International Journal of Hospitality Management, Volume 43, October 2014, Pages 1-12

    Detailed, systematic guide: HERE!

  • Oxford style

    • Oxford style citates in the body of the paper and refers to footnotes at the bottom of the paper. Each reference consists of a raised number at the end of a sentence. Footnotes provide full bibliographic details and are consecutivelly numbered throughout a chapter, a paper or a whole book (or thesis). First time citated source must be given in details and after this in a shortened version.

    In-text citation in body of the text:
    Empirical study results show that overall rating, variation and volume of consumer reviews are significantly, positively associated with hotel performance.12

    Refencing with full details of the source in footnote of the text (bellow the line):

    12 Xie, K.L. and Zhang, Z. and Zhang, Z. (2014). The business value of online consumer reviews and management response to hotel performance, in: International Journal of Hospitality Management, Volume 43, October 2014, Pages 1-12

Structure of scientific papers mainly consists of the following parts:
  1. Abstract
  2. Keywords
  3. Introduction
  4. Background and Context
  5. Literature Review
    • Literature 1
    • Literature 2
  6. Methodology
    • Methodology 1
    • Methodology 2
  7. Results
  8. Discussion and Conclusions
  9. References
